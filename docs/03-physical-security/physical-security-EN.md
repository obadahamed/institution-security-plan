# **Physical Security**

---

## 1. Objective

To prevent unauthorized physical access, intrusion, and vandalism, and to protect the organization's facilities and technological assets (such as servers and devices) from theft, damage, or environmental disasters.

---

## 2. Protected Assets

All the bank's devices and technological assets, including:

- Network devices (routers, switches, firewalls).

- Data centers and communication centers.

- ATMs
- Employee computers
- Cameras themselves

---

## 3. Threats

### 1. Unauthorized Access

- Unauthorized access by unauthorized individuals to restricted areas, equipment, or information assets (such as data centers, server rooms) without prior authorization.

### 2. Fire

- A fire or blaze inside the bank, whether intentional or accidental.

### 3. Natural Disaster

- Hurricanes, floods, or any event beyond human control.

### 4. Data Leakage via Physical Media (USB, Paper, Stolen Device)

- An attempt to transfer or steal sensitive information from a computer system using tangible, physical storage devices instead of digital network channels.

Main Physical Media Used

"Electronic supports" or physical media that can be used for data leakage include:

- **USB Flash Drives:** These are the most common method due to their ease of concealment and high data transfer speed.

- **External Hard Drives:** These are used to transfer massive amounts of data that small memory units may not accommodate.

- **Smart Devices:** Such as cell phones, MP3 players, or even smartwatches with built-in storage.

- **Compact Discs (CD/DVD):** Despite their declining use, they remain a physical medium for storing and exchanging data.

---

## 4. Controls and Solutions

### 1. Access Control:

- **Smart Cards:** Use biometric or magnetic ID cards to record entry and exit.

- **Keypads:** Digital locking systems requiring periodicly changing passwords.

- **Two-Person Rule:** Mandatory application in **server rooms,**

**encrypted asset vaults,** and **Network Control Centers (NOCs)** to ensure that no single person has unauthorized access to sensitive systems.

### 2. Monitoring:

- **CCTV Cameras:** Distribute high-definition cameras covering entrances, exits, and key corridors with continuous recording and motion detection enabled.

3. Device Protection:

- **USB Port Restriction:** Disable ports software-based or use physical port blockers to prevent data leaks.

- **Physical Backup:** Maintain copies of data on external storage media stored in **fireproof cabinets** at a separate location (off-site storage).

4. Disaster Protection:

- **Gas Suppression System:** Use clean gases such as **FM-200** or **Novec 1230**, as they extinguish fires quickly without the need for water, which can damage electronic equipment.

- **Natural Disaster Plan:** Include disaster recovery procedures such as defining roles, securing alternative power sources, and activating the backup work site to ensure business continuity.

---

5. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| --------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Senior Management:** | Is primarily responsible for setting **general policies** and allocating the necessary budgets for purchasing security systems and hiring qualified personnel. |
| **Security Department:** | Handles field operations such as guarding, camera monitoring, managing entry and exit gates, and ensuring the integrity of locks and fences. |
| **IT Department:** | Is responsible for protecting sensitive equipment (such as server rooms), securing physical device ports (such as USB ports), and controlling electronic access to facilities. |
| **Employees:** | Every employee is a "security guard" within their area of ​​responsibility; they are responsible for maintaining their equipment, not leaving storage devices (USB) in public areas, and reporting any suspicious activity. |
| **Facilities Management Department:** | Is responsible for infrastructure such as lighting distribution, fire alarm systems, and designing entrances to meet security standards. |

---

## 6. Audits

### 1. Regular Periodic Audits

- **Annually (at least):** Annual audits are the minimum internationally accepted standard, as well as standards such as **ISO 27001**, to ensure that all access controls and systems remain effective.

- **Semi-annually (every 6 months):** For sites hosting **sensitive systems** or data centers, to ensure that access logs and employee authorization changes are reviewed.

- **Every two to three years:** To conduct a comprehensive and in-depth risk assessment covering the entire infrastructure and technologies in use.

### 2. Extraordinary Audits (When Triggers Occur)

An immediate, off-schedule audit must be conducted in the following situations:

- **After a security incident:** In the event of theft, a breach, or even an unauthorized entry attempt.

- **Changes to the facility:** When moving to a new building, carrying out renovations and expansions, or changing the CCTV system and locks.

- **Changes in Organizational Structure:** In the event of mass resignations or significant management changes, a review of physical access privileges is necessary.

### 3. Technical Systems Audit (Access Check)

- **Monthly:** It is recommended to test alarm systems and surveillance cameras to ensure they are functioning correctly and free of technical malfunctions.

- **Quarterly:** Review the lists of employees with access cards and cancel inactive cards or those belonging to employees who have left the company.
