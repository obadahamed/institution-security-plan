## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to manage the risks associated with external vendors accessing the organization's data and systems, and to ensure the continuity of services while maintaining the confidentiality and integrity of information exchanged with them.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all vendors, contractors, cloud service providers, and partners who have access to the organization's data or infrastructure.


---

## 3. Policy Provisions

### - **Risk Assessment:**

A security assessment must be conducted for each supplier before contracting with them to ensure the adequacy of their security controls.

### - **Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs):**

All suppliers are required to sign a non-disclosure agreement before commencing work or being granted any access privileges.

### - **Right to Audit:**

The contract must include a clause allowing the organization (or a third party) to conduct periodic security audits of the supplier's systems to ensure compliance with agreed-upon standards.

### - **Access Control:**

Suppliers are granted specific access privileges (minimum privileges) for a limited period and are required to use multi-factor authentication (MFA). ### - **Incident Reporting:**

The supplier is obligated to immediately notify the organization (within 24 hours) of any security incident that may affect the organization's data.

### - **Termination of the Relationship:**
Upon termination of the contract with the supplier, the following must be done:

- Immediately revoke all technical and physical access privileges.

- Require the supplier to return or destroy all of the organization's data and verify this with an official destruction certificate.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Procurement Department | Ensure that security clauses are included in the signed contracts. |
| Cybersecurity Officer | Review supplier security reports and approve granting them technical access privileges. |
| Project Manager | Monitor the supplier's performance and ensure their compliance with assigned security obligations. |


---

## 5. Penalties

- A supplier's failure to comply with security standards will result in contract suspension or financial penalties.

- In the event of a data breach due to negligence, the organization has the right to terminate the contract and seek legal compensation.

--

## 6. Review

The supplier list and associated risks are reviewed annually, and contracts are updated in accordance with new regulations and laws (such as personal data protection laws).
