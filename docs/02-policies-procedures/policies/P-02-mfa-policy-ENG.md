## 1. Purpose

This policy aims to enhance the security of digital accounts and protect the organization's sensitive data from unauthorized access.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all Noor Bank employees across all departments and job levels, including new employees and external contractors with access to the bank's systems.

---

## 3. Terms and Conditions

### 3.1 **Mandatory:**
- Activating MFA is a prerequisite for accessing any technical account within the organization.

### 3.2 Approved Methods
- The use of authentication applications such as Google Authenticator, physical keys, or codes sent via official email is permitted.

### 3.3 Code Confidentiality
- Sharing authentication codes is strictly prohibited. It is recommended to use codes that change periodically and not to use static codes.

### 3.4 Exceptions
- Exceptions to the MFA policy are granted only with the written approval of the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO).

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |

| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| **IT/Security Department** | Responsible for providing the necessary tools, configuring technical settings, and providing technical support to users. |

| **Managers** | Ensuring that their teams adhere to and comply with the policy. |

| **All employees** | are responsible for setting up the MFA on their accounts. |


---

## 5. Penalties

Violating or failing to comply with this policy will result in the following actions, depending on the severity of the violation:

1- Immediate suspension of system access privileges until the situation is rectified.

2- Disciplinary action in accordance with the organization's Human Resources regulations.

3- In cases of gross negligence leading to data leaks, the penalty may be dismissal or legal prosecution.

---

## 6. Review

- This policy is reviewed **annually** by the Information Security Manager.

- It is also reviewed immediately after any security incident.
