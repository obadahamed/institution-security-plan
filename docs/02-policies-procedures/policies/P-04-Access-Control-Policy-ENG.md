## 1. Purpose

To ensure that only authorized users have access to the systems and data they need to perform their job duties, and to prevent unauthorized access to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy covers all of the organization's technology assets, including:

- Hardware (servers, computers, phones).

- Software, applications, and databases.

- Networks (wired, wireless, and VPN).

- Physical Devices

---

## 3. Terms and Conditions

### 3.1 Minimum Privilege Principle

- Users are granted only the minimum privileges necessary to perform their job duties.

### 3.2 Need to Know

- Access to information is restricted based on actual business requirements.

### 3.3 Account Management
- Each user must have a unique ID; shared accounts are strictly prohibited.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |

| --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| Data Owners | Determine the data classification level and who has access to it. |

| **IT Management** | Technically enforce privileges and immediately delete accounts of resigning or transferred employees. |

| **Employees** | Responsible for setting up their own credential protection and not disclosing passwords or leaving devices unlocked and unattended. |

| | |

---

## 5. Penalties

- Circumventing access controls or attempting to access unauthorized systems is a serious violation.

- Penalties range from written warnings to the revocation of privileges, and ultimately to termination of service and legal prosecution in cases of deliberate data leaks or system damage.

---

## 6. Review

- **Periodic Review:** User privileges are reviewed every six months to ensure their suitability for current tasks.
