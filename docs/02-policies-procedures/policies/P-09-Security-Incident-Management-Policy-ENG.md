## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents with minimal losses, while ensuring that lessons learned are documented to prevent future recurrences.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all incidents that affect information security (confidentiality, integrity, availability), whether they are technical attacks (such as viruses), human error, or physical disasters.

---

## 3. Terms and Conditions

### - **Preparation:**
Training the response team and providing the necessary tools for inspection and analysis.

### - **Detection & Analysis:**
Monitoring suspicious activity and determining the type and severity of the incident.

### - **Containment:**
Isolating infected systems immediately to prevent the spread of damage (e.g., disconnecting the server from the network).

### - **Eradication & Recovery:**
Removing the causes of the incident (deleting malware) and restoring systems from backups.

### - **Post-Incident Activity:**
Analyzing lessons learned and updating policies to address identified vulnerabilities.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Incident Response Team (IRT) | The technical officer is responsible for analyzing the incident and taking defensive actions. |
| Staff | are responsible for immediately reporting any suspicious activity (such as phishing emails or a lost device) through official channels. |
| Management | make critical decisions in major incidents (such as deciding to shut down services completely or to notify law enforcement). |


---

## 5. Penalties

- Concealing a security incident or deliberately delaying its reporting is an administrative violation that warrants accountability.

- Any attempt by an employee to conceal forensic evidence will result in dismissal and legal prosecution.

---

## 6. Review

This policy is reviewed annually, and **tabletop exercises** are conducted every six months to ensure team readiness.
