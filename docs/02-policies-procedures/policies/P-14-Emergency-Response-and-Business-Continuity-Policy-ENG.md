## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework that ensures the protection of lives, the minimization of material losses, and the continuity of the organization's vital operations in the event of a disaster (natural, technological, or security-related) and recovery as quickly as possible.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy covers all of the organization's facilities, personnel, technological systems, and core operational processes. This also includes suppliers and partners upon whom the organization relies for its continued operation.

---

## 3. Policy Provisions

### - **Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** Identify critical operations and determine the maximum allowable downtime (RTO) and maximum acceptable data loss (RPO).

### - **Disaster Recovery Plans (DRP):** Detailed technical plans must be in place to restore systems and data from alternative (off-site) locations.

### - **Alternate Work Location:** Identify alternative work locations (or enable full remote work) in case access to the main building is impossible.

### - **Crisis Communication:** Provide an up-to-date contact list for all employees, government agencies (civil defense, ambulance), and essential suppliers.

### - **Resource Readiness:** Ensure the availability of backup resources such as (power generators, alternative internet connections, off-site backups).

### - Testing & Training
- **Simulation Drills:** A physical evacuation drill and a technical systems recovery drill must be conducted at least twice a year.

- **Awareness:** All employees must be provided with a "Brief Emergency Procedures" manual and trained on how to act in the first minutes of a crisis.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Crisis Management Committee | Responsible for making major decisions, such as declaring a state of emergency and directing work teams. |
| Business Continuity Team | Responsible for implementing field and technical recovery plans. |
| Employees | Must not connect any unauthorized devices (such as personal routers) to the internal network. |

---

## 5. Penalties

- Failure to update recovery plans or to participate in mandatory drills is an administrative violation.

Any negligence on the part of the responsible teams that exacerbates the crisis or results in data loss may lead to legal accountability and strict disciplinary measures.

---

## 6. Review

This policy and its associated contingency plans will be reviewed annually, or immediately following any significant change to the infrastructure or after any actual incident, to incorporate lessons learned.
