## 1. Purpose

To define the rules and procedures necessary to create backups of critical data and systems, ensuring their recovery in the event of loss due to technical failures, cyberattacks (such as ransomware), or natural disasters.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all digital data owned by the organization, including:

- Databases and business applications.

- System and server configurations.

- User files and email correspondence.

---

## 3. Terms and Conditions

### 3.1- **Replacing Strategy (Rule 3-2-1):**
Keep **3** copies of the data, on two **2** types of media, with one **1** copy off-site.

### 3.2 **Replacing Frequency:**

- Daily backup: For variable and sensitive data.

- Weekly/Monthly backup: For less frequently changing systems and files.

### 3.3 Encryption
- All backups must be encrypted (during transit and in storage) to protect them from unauthorized access.

### Retention
- The retention period for backups is determined based on legal and operational requirements (e.g., daily backups are retained for 30 days, annual backups for 5 years). ### Testing and Validation

- **Restore Testing:** A periodic restore test must be performed (every 3 months) to ensure that backups are valid and undamaged.

- **Monitoring:** Activate an immediate alert system to notify administrators in case of any backup failure.

---

---

## 5. Penalties

- Failure to perform backups according to the specified schedule is an administrative and technical violation.

- Those responsible for data loss resulting from non-compliance with this policy are subject to legal accountability and disciplinary action based on the extent of the damage.

---

## 6. Review

- This policy is reviewed annually or upon changes to the storage infrastructure (e.g., migrating to cloud storage).
