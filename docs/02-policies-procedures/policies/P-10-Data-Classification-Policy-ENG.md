## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework for classifying the organization's data into specific categories, ensuring that appropriate protection levels are allocated to each category based on its sensitivity and the risks associated with its loss or leakage.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all data and information owned or processed by the organization, whether in **digital** (databases, electronic files) or **physical** (paper documents, printed reports).

---

## 3. Classification Levels

Data is classified into four main levels:

- **Top Secret:** The most sensitive data (e.g., trade secrets, organizational strategies, customer banking data). A leak would cause catastrophic damage.

- **Internal Use:** Data intended for use within the organization only (e.g., organizational structures, internal policies, official memos). A leak would cause embarrassment or minor operational damage.

- **Restricted:** Data accessible only to a specific group (e.g., employee payroll data, performance reports).

- **Public:** Data intended for publication (e.g., marketing materials, published annual reports, organizational news).

--
## 4. Data Handling Rules

### - **Labeling:** Documents and files must be clearly labeled to indicate their classification level (e.g., "Confidential" at the top of the page). ### - **Storage:** "Top Secret" data must be stored encrypted with access restricted by MFA authentication.

### - **Data Disposal:** "Confidential" paper documents must be destroyed using shredders, and digital data must be erased using software that prevents its recovery.

### - **Transmission:** "Confidential" data must not be sent via personal email or unencrypted storage media.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Data Owner | The manager responsible for the department that created the data. They are responsible for determining its classification level and periodically reviewing access permissions. |
| Data Custodian | Typically the IT team, they are responsible for implementing technical measures to protect the data (encryption, backup) based on the owner's classification. |
| Users | Responsible for following the handling and storage rules specific to each classification. |

---

## 5. Penalties

- Misclassifying data or handling sensitive data in a manner that violates security standards (such as leaving confidential documents in a public place) is a violation that warrants a warning or reprimand.

- Intentionally leaking data classified as "Top Secret" or as a result of gross negligence will result in termination of employment and legal prosecution.

---

## 6. Review

Data classification levels are reviewed annually, or whenever the nature of the organization's business changes, to ensure the accuracy of current classifications.
