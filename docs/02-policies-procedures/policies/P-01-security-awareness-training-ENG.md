## 1. Purpose

This policy aims to reduce human error and strengthen the security awareness of all Bank Noor employees, thereby minimizing the risks of phishing attacks, social engineering, and other human-behavior-based threats.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to **all Bank Noor employees** across all departments and job levels, including new hires and external contractors who have access to the bank's systems.

---

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Mandatory Training
- All employees must complete a mandatory information security training program at least once per year.
- New employees must complete the training within their first week of employment.

### 3.2 Role-Based Training
- Employees receive additional training tailored to their specific job role and access level.
- System administrators and high-privilege account holders must undergo advanced cybersecurity training.

### 3.3 Social Engineering Awareness
- Periodic awareness sessions are held to educate employees on attacker manipulation tactics, including trust exploitation and psychological pressure.
- Sessions include real-world examples of phishing, impersonation, and baiting attacks.

### 3.4 Attack Simulation
- Phishing simulation exercises are conducted periodically without prior notice to employees.
- Simulation results are used to identify employees who require additional training.

### 3.5 Security Commitment
- Every employee must sign a formal commitment to comply with the bank's information security policies upon hiring and at each annual review.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Party | Responsibility |
|-------|----------------|
| **Information Security Manager** | Oversee the design, execution, and compliance monitoring of training programs |
| **Human Resources Department** | Ensure new employees complete training; maintain attendance records |
| **Direct Managers** | Monitor their team's compliance with training attendance and commitment signing |
| **All Employees** | Attend mandatory training and adhere to its requirements |

---

## 5. Violations & Penalties

Non-compliance with this policy may result in the following actions, proportional to the severity of the violation:

1. **Formal warning** with mandatory re-training.
2. **Salary deduction** upon repeated violations.
3. **Financial penalty** in cases of proven deliberate negligence.
4. **Formal written apology and commitment** to senior management.
5. **Termination of employment** in cases of serious breach.
6. **Legal action** if the employee's actions result in financial or security harm to the bank or its customers.

---

## 6. Review

- This policy is reviewed **annually** by the Information Security Manager.
- It is also reviewed immediately following any security incident linked to human error.
- Training and simulation results are reviewed periodically to update program content.
