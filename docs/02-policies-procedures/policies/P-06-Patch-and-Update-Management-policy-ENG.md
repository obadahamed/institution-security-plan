## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a structured framework for identifying, testing, and installing software updates (patches) for all systems and devices in order to reduce security risks, address vulnerabilities, and improve performance stability.

---

## 2. Scope
This policy covers all of the organization's technology assets, including:

- Operating systems (Windows, Linux, macOS).

- Applications, office software, and middleware.

- - Network devices (routers, switches, firewalls).

- Database management systems and cloud platforms.

---

## 3. Terms and Conditions

### 3.1 Classification and Priorities:

Updates are classified based on their severity:

- **Critical Updates:** Installed within 24-48 hours of release.

- **Important Updates:** Installed within 14 days.

- **Optional Updates:** Installed periodically as needed.

### 3.2 Testing Phase

- Updates must be tested in an isolated environment (Test Environment) before being deployed to the production environment to ensure compatibility with existing systems.

### 3.3 Rollback Plan

A current backup and a rollback plan must be in place in case of update failure or service disruption.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **IT Admin Team** | Responsible for monitoring the release, testing, and distribution of updates to devices. |
| **Cybersecurity Team** | Responsible for vulnerability scanning to ensure the effectiveness of updates and identify systems that have not been updated. |
| **System Owners** | Responsible for setting up their credential protection and not disclosing passwords or leaving devices unlocked and unattended. |

---

## 5. Penalties

- Disabling automatic updates by employees without prior authorization is prohibited.

- Any intentional delay or negligence in implementing critical updates by the responsible team will subject them to administrative accountability, given the high risks that may result from leaving vulnerabilities open.

---

## 6. Review

- This policy will be reviewed annually to ensure its alignment with new device types and emerging cyber threats.
