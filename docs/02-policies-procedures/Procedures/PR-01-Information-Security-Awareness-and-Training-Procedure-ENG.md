## 1. Objective

To define the detailed practical steps for implementing the information security awareness and training program for all Noor Bank employees.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to the Information Security Manager, the Human Resources Department, and all line managers responsible for implementing training programs.

---

## 3. Detailed Steps

### Step 1 — Identifying Training Needs

1. Categorize employees according to their job roles (cashiers, administrators, technicians, managers).

2. Conduct a survey or assessment test to measure the current level of each category.

3. Identify gaps and weaknesses based on the survey results.

4. Document the results in a "Training Needs Assessment" report.

### Step 2 — Preparing Training Content

1. Determine the topics based on the results of Step 1 (phishing, social engineering, passwords, etc.).

2. Design customized content for each job category.

3. Schedule mandatory annual training and periodic training sessions.

4. Have the content reviewed by the Information Security Manager before implementation.

### Step 3 — Conducting Phishing Attack Simulation

1. Design a realistic phishing scenario without notifying employees.

2. Send the test email and record the results.

3. In case of employee failure — **Do not intimidate**, but immediately direct them to an educational session that explains what happened and how to respond if it happens again.

4. Document success and failure rates in the simulation report.

### Step 4 — Periodic Reminders

1. Send periodic awareness messages via internal email (monthly).

2. Post educational posters throughout the building in common areas (entrances, break rooms).

3. Issue immediate alerts when new threats or recent incidents occur in the banking sector.

### Step 5 — Post-Training Evaluation

1. Conduct a short quiz at the end of each training session to measure the acquired knowledge.

2. Analyze the results and identify employees who require additional training.

3. Conduct an annual Red Team test to measure the organization's actual readiness level.

### Step 6 — Auditing and Continuous Improvement

1. Review all training and simulation results at the end of each session.

2. Update the training content based on new threats and recorded results.

3. Submit an annual report to the Information Security Manager (ISM) including recommendations and proposed improvements.
