## 1. Purpose

To determine the "value" of information and, based on that, determine the required level of protection.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all data and information owned or processed by the organization, whether in **digital** (databases, electronic files) or **physical** (paper documents, printed reports).

---

## 3. Detailed Steps

### Step 1 — Classification Levels:

Data is divided into clear levels, usually:

- **Top Secret (Confidential/Restricted):** Data whose loss would cause serious harm (e.g., trade secrets, customer banking data).

- **Internal:** Data for employees only (e.g., company policies, organizational structure).

- **Public:** Data available to everyone (e.g., press releases, marketing brochures).

### Step 2 — Labeling:

- Add a watermark or digital seal to documents and emails to indicate their level of confidentiality.

### Step 3 — Protection by Classification:

- **Confidential Data:** Must be encrypted, prevented from being transferred to flash drives, and have a Data Leakage Prevention (DLP) system enabled.

- **Public Data:** Does not require encryption and can be freely shared. ### Step 4 — Secure Destruction Procedure

- "Confidential" documents must be shredded using shredders, and sensitive digital files must be irretrievably erased.

### Step 5 — Classification Review Procedure:

- Data must be periodically reassessed, as information may change from "confidential" to "public" over time (such as annual earnings results).

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Data Owner** | is primarily responsible for determining the classification level of data produced by their department (e.g., the Finance Manager determines the confidentiality of the budget). |
| **Information Security Manager** | is responsible for making major decisions (such as shutting down the entire company servers) and communicating with senior management. |
| **All Employees** | are obligated to classify and handle any new document they create according to confidentiality rules. |

## 6. Review

- This procedure is reviewed **annually** or immediately following any security incident related to human error.

- Any modification requires the approval of the Information Security Manager and documentation in the Change Log.
