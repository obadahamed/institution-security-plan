## 1. Objective

To establish a framework for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents with minimal losses, while ensuring that lessons learned are documented to prevent future recurrences.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all incidents that affect information security (confidentiality, integrity, availability), whether they are technical attacks (such as viruses), human error, or physical disasters.

---

## 3. Detailed Steps

These procedures follow the NIST Incident Response Life Cycle:

### Step 1 — Reporting:

- Provide a clear communication channel (extension number, email, or ticketing system) for reporting any suspicious activity.

- Require employees to report immediately if they suspect their device has been compromised or is missing.

### Step 2 — Identification & Analysis:

- Determine the type of incident (ransomware, data breach, account compromise).

- Classify the severity (low, medium, critical) to determine the response speed.

### Step 3 — Containment:

- **Short-term containment:** Immediately disconnect the infected device from the network to prevent the virus from spreading.

- **Long-term containment:** Close vulnerable ports or change the passwords of compromised systems.

### Step 4 — Eradication & Recovery:

- Remove malware and restore data from backups.

- Restore systems and ensure their integrity before allowing users to log in.

### Step 5 — Post-Incident Activity:

- Hold a "Lessons Learned" meeting to discuss the cause of the failure and how to prevent it in the future.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Entity | Responsibility |
| --------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Incident Response Team (IRT/CSIRT)** | The technical "commando" team responsible for the on-site investigation and stopping the attack. |
| **Information Security Manager** | Responsible for making major decisions (such as shutting down the entire company's servers) and communicating with senior management. |
| **All Employees** | Do not attempt to resolve the issue themselves (such as deleting files) to ensure that forensic evidence is not lost; simply report the incident. |

---

## 5. Required Documents and Records

| Document | Responsible Party |
| ------------------------------------ | ----------------------- |
| Incident Log | Response Team |
| Emergency Contact List | IT Team |
| Incident Analysis Report | Response Team |

---

## 6. Review

- This procedure is to be reviewed **annually** or immediately following any security incident related to human error.

- Any modification requires the approval of the Information Security Manager and documentation in the Change Log.
