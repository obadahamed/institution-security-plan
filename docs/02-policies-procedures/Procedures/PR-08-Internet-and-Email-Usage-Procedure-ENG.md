## 1. Purpose

To define acceptable and prohibited use of the organization's internet and email services, ensuring network protection from malware, preventing data leaks, and maintaining employee productivity.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees and contractors using the organization's technology resources, whether on-site or remotely.

---

## 3. Detailed Steps

### Step 1 — Implement Web Filtering

- Automatically block dangerous websites (such as virus, gambling, and inappropriate content sites).

- Restrict the downloading of files with dangerous extensions (such as .exe or .bat) from the internet.

### Step 2 — Implement Email Security

- Enable sandboxing to check attachments and links before they reach the inbox.

- Add an "External Tag" to messages from outside the organization to alert the employee.

### Step 3 — Implement Personal Use

- Prevent the use of official email for registering on personal websites (such as social media or shopping sites).

- Prevent linking official email to unauthorized external applications.

### Step 4 — Implement Suspicious Reporting:

- Provide a "Report Phishing" button within the employee's email to immediately report suspicions to the security team.

### Step 5 — Conducting a Surprise Audit

- Randomly check usage logs to ensure network bandwidth is not being consumed for non-work purposes (such as downloading movies).

---

## 4. Responsibilities

| Department | Responsibility |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cybersecurity Team** | Analyze reported messages and ensure they are not phishing. |
| **IT Department** | Manage servers and ensure uninterrupted email and internet service. |
| **All Employees** | Use resources responsibly and avoid opening unknown links. |
